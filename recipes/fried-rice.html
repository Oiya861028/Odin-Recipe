Fried Rice

This recipe will teach you how to make the perfect fried rice. Fried rice is a easy main course dish made with left over rice. The reason you use leftover rice is because they contain less moisture, which makes the finishing product come out looser and less clumped up. With 12 ingrediants and your own wok, you can make your own restaurant level fried rice within a few tries.

Ingrediants

  • 3/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 1/2 tablespoon oil
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten(more if you like)
  • 3 drop soy sauce
  • 3 Drops sesame oil
  • 8 ounces cooked lean pork or chicken, chopped
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped carrot(very small)
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas, thawed
  • 4 cuprs cold cooked rice
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce

Proecedures

  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in wok; add chopped onions and stir-fry until onions turn a nice brown color, about 8-10 minutes;remove from wok.
  2. Allow wok to cool slightly
  3. Mix egg with 3 drops of soy and 3 drops of sesame oil; set aside
  4. Add 1/2 tbsp oil to wok, swirling to coat surface; add egg mixture; working quickly, swil egg until egg sets against wok; when egg puff, flip egg and cook other side briefly; remove from wok, and chop into small pieces.
  5. Heat 1 tbsp oil in wok; add selected meat to wok, along with carrots peas and cooked onion; stir-fry for 2 minutes
  6. Add rice, green onions, and bean sprouts, tossing to mix well; stir-fry for 3 minutes
  7. Add 2 tbsp of light soy sauce and chopped egg to the side of rice mixture and fold in; stir-fry for 1 minute more;serve
